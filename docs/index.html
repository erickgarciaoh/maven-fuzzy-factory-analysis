02Was it luck? No — it was tested.

Four experiments, reconstructed and tested

The growth wasn't an accident of traffic. Across the three years, the site ran landing-page and checkout experiments. I reconstructed four of them from the data and put a proportion z-test on each — including the one that lost.

Assignment is inferred from disjoint date windows, not a labeled flag. Reported as a reconstruction, with a pooled-variance z-test on each overlap window.

Couldn't load the experiment chart. The full results are in the table below.

The needle-mover was the checkout test: billing vs. billing-2 lifted conversion by +17.0pp (z = 9.84), worth roughly $87K annualized. And the losing lander-4 test is shown, not buried — it cost −1.47pp at z = −3.23. Honesty is part of the evidence.

Show the work

How this was built, and what it can't tell you

  • Fictional data. Maven Fuzzy Factory is a teaching dataset with idealized patterns. This is a technique demo, not real-world market findings.
  • Inferred A/B assignment. No labeled test flag exists; arms are reconstructed from disjoint date windows and reported as such.
  • Session-scoped attribution. No cross-device stitching; each session is credited to its own entry channel.
  • Gross-margin economics only. COGS and refunds are in the data; acquisition cost is not, so there is no CAC, ROAS, or true contribution margin.
  • Annualized lifts are ceilings. They extrapolate a test window's daily rate with no seasonality — a planning ceiling, not a forecast.

The funnel percentages come straight from window functions over the pageview fact — no hand-computed steps:

-- % of step 1, and step-to-step drop, per quarter × device
CAST(sessions_reached AS DECIMAL(18,6))
  / NULLIF(FIRST_VALUE(sessions_reached)
      OVER (PARTITION BY quarter_start, device_type
             ORDER BY step_order), 0) AS pct_of_step1,
CAST(sessions_reached AS DECIMAL(18,6))
  / NULLIF(LAG(sessions_reached)
      OVER (PARTITION BY quarter_start, device_type
             ORDER BY step_order), 0) AS pct_of_prev_step
